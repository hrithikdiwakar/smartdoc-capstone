General medications and side effects of Malaria
General Medications

Common medications prescribed for Malaria:- 1. Arteether 2.Atremether 3.Artesunate 4. Bulaquine 5.Chloroquine 6.Mefloquine 7.Primaquine 8.Mepacrine 9.Proguanil 10.Tafenoquine

Side effects of general medications are

1. Arteether can cause cardiac effects such as bradycardia and QT interval prolongation.

2.Artemether side effects include Cough, diarrhea, dizziness, fatigue, headache, joint and muscle pain and stiffness, loss of appetite, nausea, stomach pain, tiredness, trouble in sleeping, vomiting, and weakness.

3.Artesunate side effects include Temporary and reversible anemia, slow heart rate, rash and fever.

4. Chloroquine,mepacrine, mefloquine side effects include

• Eye and ENT : Vision impairment, deafness, ringing in the ear and ear damage.

• Musculoskeletal : Nerve disease and muscle weakness.

• Gastrointestinal : Loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal cramps.

• Skin : Skin eruptions, pigmentation, itching, sensitivity to light, and hair loss.

• Blood : Anemia, reversible decrease in white blood cells and platelet counts.

• Central Nervous system : Seizures, headache, emotion, personality changes and depression.

• Heart : Low blood pressure.
